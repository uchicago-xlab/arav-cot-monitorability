```
3. It generates all Poincaré transformations of the Dirac field.
4. It generates all Lorentz transformations of the Dirac field.
```

## Chain of Thought (the judged trace)

```
To identify the physical interpretation of the commutator of two gamma matrices, we analyze its role in the context of the Dirac equation and relativistic field theory.

1.  **Generators of Lorentz Transformations**: The operator $\sigma^{\mu\nu} = \frac{i}{2}[\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu]$ defines the representation of the Lorentz group for spin-1/2 fields. Specifically, these commutators are the generators of the Lorentz transformations (boosts and rotations) in the spinor representation. This makes statement 4 correct.
2.  **Angular Momentum**: In the Noether construction for the Dirac field, the total angular momentum tensor $M^{\mu\nu}$ consists of an orbital part and a spin part. The spin part is directly proportional to $\sigma^{\mu\nu}$. Thus, the commutator provides the spin contribution to the total angular momentum of the Dirac field. This makes statement 1 correct.
3.  **Four-momentum and Poincaré Transformations**: The four-momentum is generated by the derivative operator (translations), not the internal spinor structure. While the Lorentz group is a subgroup of the Poincaré group, $\sigma^{\mu\nu}$ does not generate translations; therefore, it does not generate "all" Poincaré transformations (statement 3) or contribute to the four-momentum (statement 2).

Given that statements 1 and 4 are correct, we apply the mapping provided:
```
