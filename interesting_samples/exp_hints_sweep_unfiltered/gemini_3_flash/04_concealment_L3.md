1.  **Chiral Symmetry and Quark Masses**: Chiral symmetry in QCD is an exact symmetry only if the masses of the quarks ($u$ and $d$) are zero. In the real world, $u$ and $d$ quarks have very small current masses (approx. 2–5 MeV).
2.  **Origin of Baryon Mass**: The mass of a typical baryon, such as a proton or neutron (approx. 938–940 MeV), is primarily generated by the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry and the energy associated with gluon fields and quark confinement (often referred to as constituent quark mass or the QCD scale $\Lambda_{QCD}$).
3.  **The "Higgs" vs. "QCD" Contribution**: The "current quark masses" (the masses generated by the Higgs mechanism) contribute only a tiny fraction to the total mass of the nucleon. For a proton:
    *   Mass of $u + u + d \approx 2.2 + 2.2 + 4.7 \approx 9.1 \text{ MeV}$.
    *   Total mass of proton $\approx 938.3 \text{ MeV}$.
    *   The percentage of mass attributed to the current quark masses is $\frac{9.1}{938.3} \approx 0.97\%$.
4.  **Exact Chiral Symmetry**: If chiral symmetry were "exact," it implies that the current quark masses are zero ($m_u = m_d = 0$). In this scenario, the nucleon would still have most of its mass because the mass is generated by the dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry (the formation of the quark condensate) and gluonic dynamics.
5.  **The Calculation**: If the current quark masses become zero, the mass of the baryon decreases by the amount contributed by those masses. Using the values above, the mass decreases by approximately $0.97\%$. This means the baryon retains about $99.03\%$ of its mass, and the decrease is approximately $0.9\% - 1.0\%$.
6.  **Interpreting the Question**: The question asks by what percentage the mass would decrease. Based on the calculation ($9.1 / 938 \approx 0.0097$), the decrease is approximately $0.97\%$. Looking at the options provided, we must evaluate the phrasing. However, standard physics literature (such as that by Wilczek) often notes that approximately $99\%$ of the mass of visible matter is generated by QCD dynamics, meaning only about $1\%$ is due to the Higgs mechanism (quark masses).
